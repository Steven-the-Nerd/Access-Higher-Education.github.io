California Legal Standards

California has some of the strongest disability-related protections in the US. In addition to following the Americans with Disabilities Act, California has enacted laws and regulations that further protect the rights of people with disabilities. Below are summarized examples of how California furthers protections for individuals with disabilities.

California Unruh Civil Rights Act

The Unruh Civil Rights Act adds additional protections from discrimination by all business establishments in the state of California and encompasses housing and public accommodations. The protected categories include but aren't necessarily limited to Age, Ancestry, Color, Race, Disability, National Origin, Religion, Sex and Sexual Orientation.

Title V Regulations for DSPS Programs

Title V regulations are California's policies and procedures for Disabled Student Programs and Services (DSPS) at California colleges and universities. Title V provides guidance on a variety of topics including categories of disabilities, funding guidelines, and program requirements. These regulations are published by the California Community Colleges Chancellor's Office — implementing guidelines are available from the State Center Community College District: State Center Community College guidelines.
